How to Load Material Into the Vinyl Cutter

Instructions from Roland DG Corporation GS-24 User's Manual

By Mandy Harris


Step 1: Lower the Loading Lever

Lowering the loading lever releases the clamps, allowing material to be fed through the front of the vinyl cutter.

error_outline Do NOT use excessive force as this can damage the vinyl cutter.


Images from GS-24 User's Manual



Step 2: Load Your Material

Load your material through the front with the shorter edge of the material entering the vinyl cutter.



Step 3: Set A4 Material in the Position Shown Here

The loading position changes based on material size.

If needed, you may refer to the GS-24 User's Manual here for the loading positions of different material sizes.



Step 4: Move the Pinch Rollers

Move the pinch rollers to the edges of your material so that each pinch roller is within the grit marks.




Correct Pinch Roller Placement

check_circle This pinch roller is in the correct position to pull your material (media) into the vinyl cutter.


Incorrect Pinch Roller Placement

error_outline These pinch rollers are in incorrect positions.

As you can see, the pinch rollers will not be able to grab the material.



Step 5: Align the left edge of the material

Ensure the left edge of your material is lined up alongside (parallel to) the guide lines.



Step 6: Raise the Loading Lever

Once the material and grit rollers are in place, raise the loading lever.

check_circle Your material is now loaded and clamped in place!




Please note:
  • If your material is curled, bent, or creased, be sure to stretch or flatten it before use.

  • If you are loading roller material you will follow different steps. You may reference the GS-24 manual here for roller material loading instructions.
